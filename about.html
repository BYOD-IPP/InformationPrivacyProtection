Dear Prospective Participant

My name is Thendo Mbambala and I am doing research within the School of Computing at the University of South Africa. I am inviting you to participate in a study entitled "Promoting Bring Your Own Device (BYOD) Information Privacy Awareness amongst Public Libraries in Gauteng ".

What is the Purpose of the Study?

The objective of this study is to promote BYOD information privacy protection awareness among library staff and users in the Gauteng province. The primary focus of this study is using awareness material as an educational medium to increase information privacy protection. The study will include online awareness material.

Why are you being invited to participate?

You are invited to participate in the research study as you have been identified by the researcher that is enrolled with the Project 16, Module HRCOS82; at the School of Computing. The research study will consist of 100 participants.

What is the Nature of My Participation in this Study?

The study will enhance BYOD information privacy protection awareness amongst library staff and users in the Gauteng province. Your viewpoint on BYOD information privacy protection awareness will contribute to form a holistic viewpoint of BYOD information privacy protection awareness amongst the public libraries across the Gauteng region.

The study be in the form of a questionnaire. The duration will be 30 minutes and will include questions regarding BYOD information privacy protection amongst library staff and users

Can You Withdraw from this Study even after having agreed to Participate?

Participating in this study is voluntary and you are under no obligation to consent to participation. If you do decide to take part, you will be given this information sheet to keep and be asked to sign a written consent form. You are free to withdraw at any time and without giving a reason.

What are the Potential Benefits of Taking Part in this Study

The possible benefits to those who participate in this study are as follows:

  • You are given the opportunity to participate in a formal research project of the School of Computing.
  • You are given the opportunity to potentially participate in future research publications should you request to do so and should the quality of your research report warrant it.
  • You will learn more about information privacy protection and the associated risks and vulnerabilities

Are there Any Negative Consequences for me if I participate in this Research Study?

No negative consequences have been identified in the research study to date. All participation will be anonymous not coerced

Will the information that I convey to the researcher and my identity remain confidential?

You have the right to insist that your name will not be recorded anywhere and that no one, apart from the researcher and identified members of the research team, will know about your involvement in this research. Your name will not be recorded anywhere and no one will be able to connect you to the answers you give. Your answers will be given a code number or a pseudonym and you will be referred to in this way in the data, any publications, or other research reporting methods such as conference proceedings. A report of the study may be submitted for publication, but participants will not be identifiable in such a report.

How will the researcher(s) protect the security of data?

Hard copies of your answers will be stored by the researcher for a minimum period of five years in a locked cupboard/filing cabinet on UNISA premises for future research or academic purposes; electronic information will be stored on a password protected computer. Future use of the stored data will be subject to further Research Ethics Review and approval if applicable.
